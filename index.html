Logistics · Grocery Plan

Stock Up Smart

Flying in = no cooler. Here's the plan — buy one on the way.

~1:00 PM · Land & Drive
SFO
  • ✈Pick up rental car. Head east on I-580 → I-205 → Hwy 120.
No cooler? No problem. You'll grab one in ~90 min.
→
~2:30–3:00 PM · Main Stop
Manteca, CA
  • 🏬Costco — 629 E Yosemite Ave (right off Hwy 120 Exit 3). Bulk snacks, drinks, sunscreen, everything.
  • 🛒Walmart Supercenter — 1205 S Main St. Buy a $25 hard cooler + 2 bags of ice here.
  • 🥦Safeway / El Rancho — Fresh produce, deli, bakery.
💡 Pro move: Order Walmart grocery pickup online the night before (schedule for ~2:30 PM pickup). Roll in, grab it, and go.
→
~4:00 PM · Last Chance
Groveland, CA
  • 🏪Mar-Val Main Street Market — 19000 Hwy 120. Open 8 AM–8 PM. Good produce, local goods, last full grocery stop.
~1 hour from Yosemite Valley. Good for forgotten items or fresh bread.
→
~5:00 PM · Arrive
Yosemite
  • 🏔Yosemite Village Store — Emergency backup only. Convenience store prices. Open seasonally.
  • 🚫No Instacart, DoorDash, or Amazon delivery. Bears + park rules = no outside delivery services.
Food must be stored in bear boxes at all times — not in your car!
🧊
The Cooler Plan
Buy a $25–35 hard cooler at Walmart Manteca on arrival day. Pick up 2 bags of ice. It'll last 2–3 days. Restock ice at the Yosemite Village Store or any gas station in Groveland on Day 3.
📦
Pre-Order Pickup
The night before your flight, place a Walmart.com grocery order for pickup at Manteca store #1840. Schedule the pickup window for 2:30–3 PM. You land at noon — plenty of buffer. Walk in, grab your cart, done.
🐻
Bear Box Rules
All food, toiletries, and scented items must go in the bear box whenever you're away from camp or sleeping. This includes chapstick, sunscreen, and gum. Rangers will ticket you — and bears will destroy your car.

Chapter One · May 31 – June 4

YOSEMITE

3 Full Days in the Park · Arrival May 31 · Depart June 4

May 31 · Sunday · Travel Day

Arrival Day · Land ~12:10 PM · Drive ~4 hrs

First Look at the Valley

You'll roll into Yosemite around 5 PM after the drive from SFO — including your grocery stop in Manteca. Tonight is for settling in, not summiting anything. Windows down, music off. Let the valley do the talking.

  • Grocery stop in Manteca (~2:30 PM) — buy cooler + supplies
  • Check in, unpack, get camp or cabin set up
  • Short Valley View Overlook walk at dusk (15 min, flat)
  • Campfire dinner — easy first night
  • Early to bed — big days ahead
June 1 · Monday · Full Park Day

Full Day 1 · Chase the Falls

The Waterfall Tour

Yosemite has more waterfalls than almost anywhere in North America — and late spring is when they're absolute fire. Lace up and go. The roar of Yosemite Falls will stay with you forever.

  • Lower Yosemite Fall trail (easy, incredible)
  • Vernal Fall via the Mist Trail
  • Mirror Lake loop for the reflection
  • Happy Isles Nature Center (great for kids)
June 2 · Tuesday · Full Park Day

Full Day 2 · Go Big

The Adventure Day

Pick your level of epic. Permits willing, the Half Dome cables await. Not feeling cables? Glacier Point delivers the same jaw-drop in a fraction of the effort. No wrong answers today.

  • Half Dome cables (permit required — plan ahead!)
  • Glacier Point road — the ultimate panorama
  • Rock climbing intro session in the Valley
  • Valley bike rental loop for the whole family
June 3 · Wednesday · Full Park Day

Full Day 3 · The Giants

Walk Among Sequoias

These trees were alive when the Roman Empire rose and fell. The Mariposa Grove's giant sequoias don't just impress — they realign your entire sense of scale. Give this day the slow pace it deserves.

  • Mariposa Grove — 500 ancient sequoias
  • Grizzly Giant, the oldest tree in the grove
  • Wawona Meadow loop for families
  • Pioneer Yosemite History Center
June 4 · Thursday · Drive Day

Departure Day · Head to the Coast

Goodbye Mountains

Rise early, pack up camp, and squeeze in one last moment before hitting the road. Tunnel View takes 10 minutes and is the greatest goodbye in the national park system. Then point the car toward Big Sur — the Pacific is calling.

  • Pack up camp + load the car (early morning)
  • Quick stop: Tunnel View on the way out (10 min, unforgettable)
  • Coffee in Groveland — last mountain town
  • Drive south toward Big Sur via Hwy 120 → 99 → 1
  • Arrive Big Sur / Monterey area by evening
"The mountains are calling and I must go."
— John Muir

June 5 · The Road Between

HIGHWAY 1

The world's most spectacular coastal road connects your mountains to the sea. Big Sur cliffs plunge into the Pacific. Pull over constantly. You'll want to.

Yosemite → Merced → Carmel → Big Sur → Monterey

Chapter Two · June 6

MONTEREY

The Pacific · Cannery Row · Carmel-by-the-Sea

June 6 · Friday

Arrive · Monterey Bay

Hello, Pacific

Trade your trail dust for sea salt. Monterey Bay greets you with crashing waves, barking sea lions, and Cannery Row's irresistible energy. Drop the bags and start exploring — you've earned it.

  • Monterey Bay Aquarium (world class — don't skip it)
  • Cannery Row stroll at sunset
  • Fisherman's Wharf for clam chowder in a bread bowl
  • 17-Mile Drive through Pebble Beach
Explore · Carmel

Day Trips · Carmel & Beyond

Carmel-by-the-Sea

A storybook village just minutes from Monterey — no street addresses, no Starbucks, no chain stores. Just galleries, fairy-tale cottages, world-class restaurants, and a white sand beach that feels like the edge of the world.

  • Carmel Beach — stunning white sand
  • Point Lobos State Reserve — seals & otters
  • Bixby Bridge viewpoint on Highway 1
  • Wine tasting in Carmel Valley
