Logistics · Grocery Plan

Stock Up Smart

Flying in = no cooler. Here's the plan — buy one on the way.

~12:30 PM · Depart SFO
SFO
  • ✈Pick up rental car. Head east on I-580 → I-205 → CA-99 S. Manteca is your first stop — right at the junction.
Route: SFO → I-580 → CA-99 S → Manteca → CA-99 S → Madera → Hwy 41 N → Oakhurst → Yosemite Forks
→
~2:30 PM · Big Chain Stop
Manteca, CA
  • 🏬Costco — 629 E Yosemite Ave (Hwy 120 junction, Exit 3 off CA-99). Large dedicated gluten-free section. Bulk GF snacks, pasta, bread, bars.
  • 🛒Walmart Supercenter — 1205 S Main St. Buy the cooler + ice here. Wide GF product range at the best prices.
💡 Best spot for gluten-free needs — chain store scale means reliable GF labeling and dedicated sections. Pre-order Walmart pickup for 2:30 PM the night before your flight.
→
~3:45 PM · Fresh Top-Up
Oakhurst, CA
  • 🛒Vons — 40044 Hwy 49 (at Hwy 41). Open 5 AM–midnight. Good for fresh produce, deli, and any GF items Manteca didn't have.
  • 🥦Raley's — Directly across the intersection. Strong natural/organic selection with GF options.
Your local store for the whole stay — only 15 min from Yosemite Forks. Easy to pop back any morning.
→
~5:00 PM · Arrive
Yosemite Forks
  • 🏠Check in, unpack, stock the fridge. 10 miles from the South Entrance.
  • 🦌Bass Lake is minutes away for a quick sunset walk.
No in-park delivery (bears + park rules). Oakhurst is your resupply point throughout the stay.
🌾
Gluten-Free Strategy
Do your main GF haul at Manteca Costco or Walmart — both have large, clearly labeled gluten-free sections with the best selection and prices. Restock specifics at Oakhurst Raley's (strong natural foods) during your stay. Avoid relying on in-park stores — limited and expensive.
🧊
The Cooler Plan
Buy a $25–35 hard cooler at Walmart Manteca on arrival day — best price on the route. Grab 2 bags of ice. Since you're at a house/rental you'll have a fridge too, so the cooler is mainly for packed lunches on park days.
🍽️
Oakhurst Eats
Oakhurst has real restaurants at non-park prices — great for arrival night dinner and morning coffee before early park days. When eating out, call ahead about GF options; Oakhurst restaurants are generally accustomed to dietary requests.

Chapter One · May 31 – June 3

YOSEMITE

2 Full Days in the Park · Based in Yosemite Forks · 10 mi from South Entrance · Depart June 3

May 31 · Sunday · Travel Day

Arrival Day · Land 12:10 PM · Two Grocery Stops En Route

Settle In, Rest Up

From SFO, head east on I-580 → CA-99 south. First stop: Manteca for the big haul — Costco or Walmart for gluten-free supplies and your cooler. Then continue south on CA-99 to Hwy 41 north through Oakhurst for any fresh items before landing at Yosemite Forks around 5 PM.

  • Stop 1: Manteca (~2:30 PM) — Costco or Walmart, GF haul + buy cooler
  • Stop 2: Oakhurst (~3:45 PM) — Vons or Raley's for fresh produce
  • Arrive Yosemite Forks ~5 PM — check in, unpack, stock fridge
  • Sunset walk at Bass Lake — just minutes away
  • Early to bed — heading into the park by 8 AM tomorrow
June 1 · Monday · Full Park Day

Full Day 1 · Waterfalls + Golden Hour · 45 min to the Valley

The Waterfall Tour

Leave Yosemite Forks by 8 AM and you're in the valley by 9. Late spring waterfalls are at absolute peak — snowmelt is still roaring. Spend the day on the valley floor, then end with golden hour at Panorama Point before the drive back.

  • Enter via South Entrance (Hwy 41) — depart by 8 AM
  • Lower Yosemite Fall trail — easy, flat, all ages
  • Vernal Fall footbridge (Mist Trail) — go as far as the group wants
  • Mirror Lake loop — stroller-friendly, stunning Half Dome reflection
  • Happy Isles Nature Center — perfect for the little ones
  • ★ Golden hour: Panorama Point via Glacier Point Rd — don't skip this
June 2 · Tuesday · Full Park Day

Full Day 2 · Giants, Views & Easy Wins · All Ages

Big Trees, Big Views

The perfect family day — no big hikes required. Mariposa Grove is just 15 minutes from your door and completely blows kids' minds (and adults'). Glacier Point is a short drive for the most dramatic view in the park. Valley floor biking rounds out the afternoon for whoever has energy left.

  • Mariposa Grove — 15 min from base, go early before crowds
  • Grizzly Giant + California Tunnel Tree — kids walk through a tree
  • Glacier Point — drive up, 5 min walk, jaw-dropping panorama
  • Valley floor loop by bike — rentals available, great for ages 4+
  • Cook's Meadow stroll — flat, paved, stroller-friendly, deer everywhere
  • Curry Village pool (seasonal) — best reward for tired little legs
June 3 · Wednesday · Drive Day

Departure Day · Yosemite Forks → Big Sur

Goodbye Mountains

Check out of Yosemite Forks and head south on Hwy 41 — you're already pointed the right direction. A quick stop at Tunnel View on the way through the park is the greatest goodbye in the national park system. Then it's Fresno → the 99 → and eventually the Pacific.

  • Check out, pack the car — early morning
  • Optional: Tunnel View on the way through the valley
  • Drive south via Hwy 41 → Fresno → CA-99 → coast
  • Lunch stop in Fresno or along the way
  • Arrive Big Sur area by evening

Chapter Two · June 4–5

MONTEREY

The Pacific · Cannery Row · Carmel-by-the-Sea

June 4 · Thursday

Arrive · Big Sur → Monterey Bay

Hello, Pacific

Trade your trail dust for sea salt. Drive the legendary Highway 1 through Big Sur before rolling into Monterey Bay. Cannery Row and crashing waves greet you. Drop the bags and start exploring.

  • Bixby Bridge overlook on Highway 1
  • McWay Falls — waterfall straight into the ocean
  • Arrive Monterey · Cannery Row stroll at sunset
  • Fisherman's Wharf — clam chowder bread bowl
June 5 · Friday

Full Day · Carmel & Beyond

Carmel-by-the-Sea

Your one full day on the Pacific. Start at the Aquarium, then lose yourselves in Carmel's storybook streets. End with the golden light along 17-Mile Drive. It's the kind of day you talk about for years.

  • Monterey Bay Aquarium — world class, don't skip it
  • Carmel Beach — stunning white sand
  • Point Lobos State Reserve — seals & sea otters
  • 17-Mile Drive through Pebble Beach at golden hour
